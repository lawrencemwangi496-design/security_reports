# OpenClaw Security Report — 2026-03-27

## Verdict: FAIL

## Summary
The scan results indicate the presence of several HIGH and CRITICAL severity vulnerabilities in the dependencies, specifically in the fast-xml-parser, jws, and node-forge packages. These vulnerabilities can lead to security issues such as cross-site scripting, denial of service, and improper signature verification. Furthermore, there are misconfigurations detected in the Dockerfiles, including the lack of a HEALTHCHECK instruction and the use of an unspecified tag in the 'FROM' statement.

## Critical Findings
- [CRITICAL] fast-xml-parser via Trivy
  Issue: CVE-2026-25896: Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) due to improper DOCTYPE entity handling
  Fix: Update to version 5.3.5 or 4.5.4

## Warnings
- CVE-2026-22610 in @angular/compiler and @angular/core
- CVE-2026-26278 in fast-xml-parser
- CVE-2025-65945 in jws
- CVE-2025-12816 in node-forge

## Recommendation
Due to the presence of CRITICAL severity vulnerabilities with available fixes, it is strongly recommended to update the affected dependencies to the latest secure versions before deploying the code. Additionally, it is advised to address the misconfigurations detected in the Dockerfiles by adding a HEALTHCHECK instruction and specifying a tag in the 'FROM' statement to ensure the security and reliability of the containerized application. The code should not be deployed until these issues are resolved to prevent potential security breaches and maintain the integrity of the system.
